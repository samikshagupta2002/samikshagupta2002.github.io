Projects

  • 2023

    3D Printing of Lens

    Ottawa, ON

    This project, conducted as part of the Optoelectronics Devices Labs course (OSS2008) at Algonquin College, delves into the realm of 3D printing applications in optics. Under the guidance of Professor Dr. Ilya Golub, the experiment aims to identify suitable 3D printers and materials for lens production. The focus is on designing and 3D printing a plano-convex lens with specific parameters, utilizing transparent filaments and adhering to safety procedures.

  • 2023

    Manufacturing a Y-coupler

    Ottawa, ON

    In the Optics/Optical Fibers I course at Algonquin College, a hands-on project was undertaken under the guidance of Dr. Wahab Almuhtadi, focusing on the design, manufacturing, and testing of a 50/50 Y-coupler. The project involved the execution of precise steps, including the cutting of fiber optic cables, the application of heat shrink tubing, the incorporation of metal tubes with epoxy, and the execution of thorough polishing. Meticulous testing, utilizing a laser source and microscope, was carried out to verify the Y-coupler's performance and gain practical insights into optical principles. This project, serving as both a self-sufficient product and a testing tool, significantly deepened the understanding of optics and equipped participants with essential skills for future endeavors in optical engineering.
